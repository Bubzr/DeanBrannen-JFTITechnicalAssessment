Hello, my name is Dean Brannen, a software developer currently looking for professional work. Technical writing is important as it pertains very deeply to my position at any company I will work for given my background and experience. The documentation that goes into any piece of software is the deciding factor between a groundbreaking tool and a frustrating application that is too complex to make teaching a whole office worth the time of the company. The process of technical writing is a very important aspect among every member of an engineering team. All too often I hear a joke from fellow developers, "Only god and I know how my code works". While humorous, this sentiment reflects a real and costly problem in the industry; code that only one person understands becomes a liability the moment that person is unavailable, and it is precisely this problem that a mindset of thorough documentation and technical writing can solve. The best example I can think of in my own experience is the struggle between the Unity and Unreal game engine. Unity's documentation is widely regarded as far more accessible and thorough than Unreal Engine's, and as a result it has become many developers' default choice, mine included, not necessarily because of the engine itself, but because of how well it is understood by the community and the developers behind them. For these reasons I hold documentation to the same standard as the code itself. it is more than a courtesy, it's a responsibility to every developer who will use the project after me.
